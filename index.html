Momos are a popular type of South Asian dumpling, consisting of a thin, dough-based wrapper filled with various ingredients. They are considered a staple comfort food, known for their soft texture, juicy filling, and savory taste

ingredients

  • All-purpose Flour (Maida): 2 cups
  • Salt: ½ teaspoon
  • Oil: 1 tablespoon (makes the dough smooth)
  • Water: As needed (add gradually to make a firm, non-sticky dough)
  • Tomatoes: 3-4 large (with a small "X" cut on top for boiling)
  • Dried Red Chillies: 8-10 (soaked in hot water for 15 mins for heat)
  • Garlic: 10-12 cloves (generous amount for the signature pungent taste)
  • Ginger: ½ inch piece
  • Vinegar: 1 tablespoon
  • Soy Sauce: ½ tablespoon (for depth and color)
  • Salt & Sugar: To taste (sugar helps balance the extreme heat)

INSTRUCTION

  • Step 1: Prepare the Dough (The Wrapper)
  • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour (maida) and a pinch of salt.
  • Add water gradually and knead it into a firm/stiff dough. (Do not make it soft like roti dough).
  • Apply a teaspoon of oil over the dough, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rest for 30 minutes. This makes the dough stretchy.
  • Step 2: Prepare the Filling (The Stuffing)
  • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add finely chopped garlic, ginger, and green chillies. Sauté for a minute.
  • Add your finely shredded vegetables (cabbage, carrots, onions) or minced chicken
  • Cook on high heat for 2–3 minutes to remove excess moisture. Note: Vegetables should stay crunchy.
  • Season with salt, black pepper, and soy sauce. Turn off the heat and let the mixture cool completely.
  • Step 3: Shaping the Momos
  • Take a small ball of dough (the size of a marble) and roll it into a very thin circle (about 3–4 inches wide).
  • Pro Tip: Keep the edges thinner than the center so the pleats don't taste doughy
  • Place a spoonful of filling in the center.
  • Apply a little water to the edges. Lift one side and start making small pleats (folds), joining them together to seal the momo into a pouch or half-moon shape.
  • Step 4: Steaming
  • Grease your steamer plate with a little oil so the momos don't stick.
  • Arrange the momos on the plate, leaving some space between them as they expand slightly.
  • Steam over boiling water on medium-high heat for 10 to 12 minutes
  • The momos are ready when they look translucent and feel non-sticky to the touch.
  • Step 5: The Spicy Sauce (Chutney)
  • Grind the boiled tomatoes and red chillies with garlic, ginger, salt, and vinegar into a smooth paste.
  • For a street-style finish, you can sauté this paste in a little oil for 2 minutes.
  • Serve your hot steamed momos with the spicy red sauce!
